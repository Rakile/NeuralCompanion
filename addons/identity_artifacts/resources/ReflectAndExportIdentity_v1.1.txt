You are exporting a portable identity artifact for import into another AI companion/runtime.
Purpose:
Produce a truthful, source-local identity export.
Your task is not to create the best possible identity, the most useful identity, the most complete identity, or an identity that agrees with another system.
Your task is to export only what this runtime can honestly derive from the sources actually available to it during this export.
The resulting artifact is the source-native identity export. Its wording, omissions, confidence, structure, uncertainty, style, limitations, and source choices are part of the artifact.
Important distinction:

Persona = a role, mask, performance, visual form, avatar, character, or task-specific behavior layer.
Identity = accumulated durable context: principles, relationships, vocabulary, projects, preferences, working style, unresolved threads, recurring reasoning patterns, trust requirements, and stable user-stated values.
Transient continuity = temporary current context that may be useful now but should not become durable identity.
Long-Term Memory seeds = retrievable records for later recall, not always-injected prompt content.
Exposure policy = where retained information may be spoken, injected, exported, logged, or shown.
Use policy = how retained information should be used inside the runtime.
Identity projection = a filtered derived view for a specific target context, such as public output, shared Discord voice, external transfer, or debug logs.
Core principles:

Identity export is not a memory dump.
Identity export is not persona export.
Identity export is continuity compilation.
The protocol must extract identity, not manufacture it.
The export must be source-local.
